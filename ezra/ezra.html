Hi! My name is Ms. Ezra

Ezra Belgrave is a fourth year student at Duke University! She is passionate about education, Computer Science, and accessibility. She love using CS as a tool for social change and a way to express creativity!

Born and raised in Philadelphia, Ezra is a first-generation college student of Afro-Latina descent. Ezra lives in Durham with her two dogs and cat.

Where oh where could she be? Ezra's favorite side hobby is photography and story telling. She began photography classes during her 2nd year at Duke under Susie Post-Rust. This upcoming fall, she will be an assistant teacher for a Documentary Studies photography course taught by Post-Rust. Her past works include stories about Black Cowboys in the USA, the history of Durham School of the Arts, Black Joy, and much more!

Ezra loves to keep busy on campus. She is a goaltender for Duke Women's Club Lacrosse and Men's Ice Hockey. Outside of practice, Ezra also serves as the Coeditor in Chief for KHAYA Magazine, a Black magazine for students on Duke's campus.

Music on my mind

Ms. Belgrave

Khaya Magazine was founded in Spring 2020 and is the first African magazine on campus. For me and so many other Black students on Duke’s campus, Khaya is more than a magazine production. Khaya has been a home away from home, a place where we feel loved, accepted, and celebrated. With my photos, I wanted to show the pure magic in Black joy. I want to give a big thanks to the executive staff at Khaya: Azana Green, Lydia Sellers, Sydney Reed, Tiana Horace, Urena Okoye, and James Mbuthia.
